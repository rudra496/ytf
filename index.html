The Power of Small Actions

Every decision you make ripples through your community. Here is how RippleUp turns small efforts into habits.

1. Scan & Verify

Scan vivid QR codes at water refill stations, recycling hubs, local green vendors, and public transport zones to log your sustainable actions instantly.

2. Track Impact

Visualize your personal contributions in real-time, including exact plastic bottles avoided, food waste prevented, and carbon footprint (CO₂) reductions.

3. Unlock Rewards

Redeem points for discounts at local sustainable vendors, coupons for eco-friendly products, badges, and recognition certificates.

Climate Insights & Research

Understanding the foundation, findings, and stakeholder interviews behind RippleUp.

The UNICEF-IUCN Context

According to joint research by UNICEF and the IUCN, youth in Least Developed Countries have a strong, innate motivation to participate in climate resilience actions. However, they face a critical barrier: **the lack of structured opportunities, incentives, and accessible local channels to act.**

Traditional environmental platforms rely heavily on raising passive awareness, which fails to drive actual, sustained habit formation. RippleUp bridges this gap by introducing physical-plus-digital interventions (QR stickers placed directly at water-refill stations and local produce markets) and micro-incentives to turn intention into daily routine.

"The low adoption and retention of climate-conscious behaviors among urban youth in Least Developed Countries is driven by a combination of inadequate green infrastructure, unsupportive urban planning, and ineffective digital behavior-change solutions." — Team #12 Problem Definition

Stakeholder Insights

Ridwan Syed Saad, 25 Student, Savar

"I think all three problems are important, but vehicle pollution and outdoor heat are the most critical because they directly affect daily life and choices. For me and many others, extreme heat makes it very difficult to choose walking, cycling, or public transport. I'd scan a colorful QR code at a water station if it looked fun, quick, and clearly showed I'd get points or impact tracking."

Tanvir Ahmed, 20 Daily Cyclist

"Climate action ideas should explore how sustainable living can emerge naturally from the everyday lives of youth, without assuming fixed solutions. If I could see my progress building toward something—like points bar filling up—that would make me come back and scan it again next week. Discounts are very encouraging."
