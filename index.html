The Power of Small Actions

Every decision you make ripples through your community. Here is how RippleUp turns small efforts into habits.

1. Scan & Verify

Scan vivid QR codes at water refill stations, recycling hubs, local green vendors, and public transport zones to log your sustainable actions instantly.

2. Track Impact

Visualize your personal contributions in real-time, including exact plastic bottles avoided, food waste prevented, and carbon footprint (CO₂) reductions.

3. Unlock Rewards

Redeem points for discounts at local sustainable vendors, coupons for eco-friendly products, badges, and recognition certificates.

Climate Insights & Research

The background, surveys, validation stats, and stakeholder interviews behind the RippleUp platform.

1. Our Why: Problem & Audience

Despite rising awareness, sustainable habit loops among urban youth in developing countries remain inconsistent. Intention exists, but reward loops and supporting infrastructure are absent.

Would adopt green habits if rewarded 77.3%
Identify poor infrastructure as top barrier 63.6%
Prefer gamified digital habit tracking 70.0%

Global Toll: 1.05 billion tonnes of annual food waste (8–10% of global emissions) & 20 million metric tonnes of plastic environmental leakage.

2. Our What: The Platform Loop

RippleUp gamifies eco-habits without requesting drastic lifestyle shifts. By refilling bottles, recycling, or choosing local produce, users verify actions at physical stations to earn instant feedback.

  • 1 Choose Action: Refill, recycle, walking.
  • 2 Scan QR: Verify action at vendor/hub.
  • 3 Earn Points: Immediate gamified rewards.
  • 4 Redeem: Local green vendor coupons.

Build Feasibility: Ready for real-world validation via QR stickers, custom app simulator frontends, and community partnerships.

3. Our Impact: Habit Loops

Our qualitative interviews confirmed that youth are significantly more likely to continue green behaviors when they are simple, visually tracked, and integrated.

>70%
Express direct preference for digital habit tracking with points.
Ridwan, 25: "Vehicle pollution and outdoor heat are critical. I'd scan a colorful QR code at a water station if it was fun, quick, and tracked my impact."
Tanvir, 20: "Eco living should emerge naturally. Seeing my points bar fill up and unlocking discounts encourages me to come back."

4. Our Journey: Ideate Pivot

We began with a broad goal to address multiple climate challenges. Through mentor feedback, we successfully pivoted to a focused, high-retention platform centered on physical QR codes.
